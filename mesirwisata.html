Gyeongbokgung Palace

Established shortly after the foundation of the Joseon Dynasty in 1392, Gyeongbokgung Palace was the first royal palace built in Seoul. The name "Gyeongbokgung" translates to "Palace of Shining Happiness." Throughout its history, the palace has undergone multiple reconstructions due to damages from wars and occupations. Notably, it was destroyed during the Imjin War in 1592 and later reconstructed in the late 19th century under King Gojong's reign.

Address: 161 Sajik-ro, Sejongno, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 18:00
Price: 1.000 KRW (Children) and 3.000 KRW (Adults)

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon Hanok Village is a historic neighborhood in Seoul, South Korea, renowned for its concentration of traditional Korean houses known as hanok. Situated between Gyeongbokgung Palace, Changdeokgung Palace, and Jongmyo Shrine, the village offers a glimpse into the architectural and cultural heritage of the Joseon Dynasty (1392–1897).

Address: 37 Gyedong-gil, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free to walk around the villages
