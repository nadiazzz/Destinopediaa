Halong Bay

Halong Bay is one of Vietnam's most iconic destinations, located in Quang Ninh Province, about 170 km
northeast of Hanoi. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Halong Bay has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1994 and is recognized as one of the New 7 Natural Wonders of the World.

Address: Quang Ninh Province of Hanoi, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Start from 680.000 VND

Hoi An Ancient Town

Hoi An Ancient Town is a beautifully preserved UNESCO World Heritage Site located in central Vietnam. Once a bustling trading port, the town is now famous for its charming lantern-lit streets, historic architecture, and rich cultural heritage.

Address: Hoi An, Quang NAm Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 17:00 (The main area)
Price: Start from 120.000 VND

War Remnants Museum

Established in 1975, shortly after the end of the Vietnam War, the museum was originally named the "Exhibition House for US and Puppet Crimes." Over time, reflecting the normalization of diplomatic relations between Vietnam and the United States, it was renamed to the War Remnants Museum in 1995.

Address: Ho Chi Minh City, District 3, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:30 - 18:00
Price: 40.000 VND (Adult) / 20.000 VND (Children) / Free (Under 6)

Reunification Palace

Reunification Palace (also known as Independence Palace or Dinh Độc Lập) is a significant historical landmark in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. It served as the residence and workplace of the President of South Vietnam during the Vietnam War and became a symbol of the country's reunification.

Address: 135 Nam Ky Khoi Nghia Street, Ben Thanh Ward, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 16:00
Price: 65.000 VND (Adult) / 15.000 VND (Children)

Cu Chi Tunnels

Cu Chi Tunnels are an extensive network of underground tunnels located about 70 kilometers northwest of Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. These tunnels were used by the Viet Cong during the Vietnam War as hiding spots, communication routes, and living quarters.

Address: 70 km from Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 17:00
Price: Start from 90.000 VND

Sa Pa Terraces

Sa Pa Terraces in northern Vietnam are among the world's most breathtaking agricultural landscapes. Carved into the mountainsides by ethnic minority groups such as the Hmong and Dao, these terraced rice fields are a testament to centuries of harmonious coexistence between humans and nature.

Address: The Sa Pa Terraces are spread across the Muong Hoa Valley, near Sa Pa town, Lao Cai Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Start from 150.000 VND (Adult) / Children : Free

Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park

Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Quảng Bình Province, Vietnam, is renowned for its vast karst landscapes and extensive cave systems, including the world’s largest cave, Sơn Đoòng. The park's diverse ecosystem features tropical forests, underground rivers, and rare wildlife, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

Address: Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park, Sơn Trạch Commune, Bố Trạch District, Quảng Bình Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 17:00
Price: Start from 150.000 VND

Imperial City of Huế

The Imperial City of Huế, located in central Vietnam, served as the political and cultural heart of the Nguyễn Dynasty from 1802 to 1945. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its grand palaces, ornate temples, and imposing gates, all enclosed within massive stone walls and a protective moat.

Address: 23/8 Street, Thuan Hoa Ward, Huế City, Thừa Thiên Huế Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 17:30
Price: Start from 200.000 VND (Adult) / Children : Free

Temple of Literature

The Temple of Literature (Văn Miếu – Quốc Tử Giám) in Hanoi, Vietnam, is a revered historical site dedicated to Confucius and Vietnam's first national university, established in 1070. This well-preserved complex features traditional Vietnamese architecture, including five courtyards, ancient pavilions, and stone steles inscribed with the names of scholars who passed royal examinations.

Address: 58 Quốc Tử Giám Street, Văn Miếu Ward, Đống Đa District, Hanoi, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 17:00
Price: Start from 35.000 VND

My Son Sanctuary

Constructed between the 4th and 13th centuries by the Champa civilization, these temples were dedicated to the worship of Shiva and served as a spiritual and political center. Nestled in a lush valley surrounded by mountains, the sanctuary showcases intricate red-brick structures and stone carvings, reflecting a fusion of indigenous and Indian architectural influences.

Address: Duy Phu Commune, Duy Xuyen District, Quang Nam Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 06:00 - 18:00
Price: Start from 150.000 VND (Adult) / Children: Free
